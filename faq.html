  • Q: Do I need to take an Adult Driver Education course?

     

    The state requires that a candidate (adult 18-20 years old first time driver) applies for a driver license needs to take and pass an Adult Driver Education course.

     

  • Q: What is the requirement to pass the course?

     

    You must complete within 30 days after started (after payment). The final exam consists of 16 multi-choice questions and 4 True or False questions. You just need to be correct for 15 questions. You are allowed to take the exam two times.

     

  • Q: After I go the certificate, how to get my driver’s license? (if you are 18-20 years old)

     

    If you 18-20 years old and got the certificate, you will need behind-the- wheel practice (need an instructional permit to practice), and then take the driver’s license test at a DMV. The instructional permit can be obtained after your written test at a DMV. Our school can help you prepare the test. Our school can help you the behind-the- wheel training also.

     

  • Q: How long is the online course?

     

    This course is 6 hours.

  • Q: How do I get my Illinois driver's license?

     

    Adult drivers ages 18 to 20 who are applying for an Illinois driver license for the first time and who have never completed a driver education course before will need to take a 6-hour Adult Driver Education course in order to obtain their first license. Students can begin the Adult Driver Ed course as early as 17 and 3 months to apply for a license when they are 18.

  • Q: Do I need an Instruction Permit?

     

    An Illinois Instruction Permit (or learner's permit) is not required for adults 18 and older to apply for their license, but it is the only way to receive behind-the-wheel practice before taking the Illinois driving test. Adults ages 17 years and 3 months or older can apply for an instruction permit without taking a driver ed course, and since those students will be applying for their license after they turn 18, are also eligible to complete the Adult Driver Ed course.

     

    If you have already received your Instruction Permit, you will need to submit a scanned copy to us before you can complete your Adult Driver Ed course. Don't worry - we make the process nice and easy and explain it all during your course!

     

  • Q: If I am over 20 years old, how can I get my driver’s license?

     

    If you are over 20 years old, you are not required to take the Adult Driver Education course to get a driver’s license. You need to pass a driving test at a DMV to get a driver’s license. But you still need to get an Instructional Permit (need a written test and vision test), before take the driving test at a DMV. Our school can help you prepare the tests. Our school can help you the behind-the-wheel training also.

     

  • Q: Do you report my completion directly to the Secretary of State?

     

    Yes, we will report your completion to the Secretary of State, who will issue your certificate.

  • Q: When will I receive my certificate?

     

    Once you have completed your course, we will report your completion to the Secretary of State, who will issue your certificate to you via e-mail. Once you receive the e-mail from the state, you will need to print your completion certificate from the state website and bring the certificate to the Secretary of State (DMV) location when you apply for your license.

  • Q: Contents of the course?

     

    Based on the state requirement, the course Objectives are: The educational objectives of adult driver education include, but not be limited to, promoting respect for and encouraging observance of traffic laws and traffic safety responsibilities of drivers and citizens; reducing traffic violations; reducing traffic-related injuries, deaths and economic losses; and motivating continuing development of traffic related competencies through education, including, but not limited to, Illinois traffic law, risk management, driver attitudes, courtesy skills, and informing participants about the effects of alcohol and other drugs on driving ability.
